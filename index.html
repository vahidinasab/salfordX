Our mission

Research that enables self-sustaining communities and businesses

Salford-X advances practical research on citizen-led flexibility, vehicle-to-everything (V2X), digital twins for energy and markets, and dynamic life-cycle assessment. We design, test, and scale solutions that help deliver the UK’s clean power goals by 2030, while protecting consumers and improving local resilience.

Themes

What we work on

Citizen-led flexibility

Market design, consumer protections, distribution-system coordination, and settlement models that value local flexibility fairly.

V2X and EV systems

Smart charging and bidirectional operation, state-of-charge forecasting, fairness metrics, and fleet-level trials across homes, business sites, and public locations.

Digital twins

Grid-edge and market twins that couple power-flow models with market clearing and constraints, supporting planning, dispatch, and verification.

Dynamic LCA and circularity

Time-varying carbon accounting for assets and services, with links to product and place-based labelling and industrial symbiosis.

Objectives

2025-2027 research objectives

  • Demonstrate consumer-centred flexibility that is measurable, auditable, and simple to adopt for households, SMEs, and public sites.
  • Deploy and evaluate V2X services that support bill savings and local network security, with evidence from real pilots.
  • Release open tools and data for market-grid coupling, smart charging, and digital-twin analytics, suitable for education and replication.
  • Co-create policy-aligned blueprints with DSOs, market bodies, and communities for fair scaling of flexibility.

Policy alignment

How our work aligns with UK strategy

Clean Power 2030

We target research that supports the national push for clean power by 2030, including better planning, connections reform, and consumer outcomes.

Clean Flexibility Roadmap

Our designs support a consumer-focused flexibility system and contribute to approaches that increase clean flexibility capacity at speed.

Market facilitation

Our work on data, settlement, and digitalisation complements UK market-facilitator actions to integrate distributed flexibility efficiently.
