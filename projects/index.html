Pilots

Real-world demonstrations

We run and support pilots that connect research with practice. Each pilot has a clear learning plan, open artefacts where possible, and a route for replication with public and private partners.

Isle of Wight – V2B and V2G sites

Part of the EU Horizon DriVe2X programme, these sites test bidirectional EV charging in hotels, public car parks, and community locations. We evaluate consumer journeys, operational reliability, energy and bill impacts, and network support.

What we test

Availability and state-of-charge forecasting, charge-discharge scheduling, user incentives, and interoperability.

What we measure

Peak-shaving, self-consumption, emissions intensity by time, and value captured by site owners and drivers.

Why it matters

Evidence for scaling V2X in tourism and mixed-use communities where parking dwell times are favourable.

Salford campus – local flexibility and V2G

A campus pilot exploring market-grid coupling, fairness in dispatch, and settlement visibility, with a bidirectional charger integrated into smart-charging control. The site is used to test demand response with real operational constraints.

Focus

Nodal or locational pricing signals at the grid edge, consumer protections, and operational cyber-safety.

Outputs

Open datasets, code releases, and practical guidance for estates and fleet managers.

Partners

University of Salford teams with local and national collaborators. New partners are welcome.

MGX – market-grid exchange demonstrator

An open demonstrator that links distribution-network constraints with a local market clearing engine. It supports experimentation with consumer neutrality windows, fairness metrics, and settlement checks, and provides a learning path for DSOs and communities.

Work with us

We co-create pilots with local authorities, DSOs, market bodies, SMEs, and community groups. Get in touch to propose a site or to adopt our tools and methods.
